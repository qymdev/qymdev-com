PrayerCount

A small companion for your salah, made to disappear into the practice itself.

✦   ✦   ✦

PrayerCount keeps track of the rakats of your daily salah using nothing but your Apple Watch. The watch's barometric altimeter senses when you bow into sujood and lift again into qiyam, and that quiet difference in the air is enough to advance the count. A gentle haptic at the final rakat lets you finish without ever turning your wrist toward your eyes — no glance, no tap, no breaking of attention.

Everything stays on the watch. No iPhone is required during prayer. There is no account to create, no server to call, no analytics that watch you back, no advertising. The app is built by one person, sold for nothing, and kept honest by the simplest design choice available: do not collect what you do not need.

“The work is between you and your watch. Nothing leaves the wrist.” The principle, plainly

✦

— I —The Practice

Begin a prayer with a single tap, choose between Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha, Witr, or a custom 2- or 4-rakat session for nafl and sunnah. The app suggests the right prayer for the time and your location. From there it watches, listens to its sensors, and counts.

The watch knows when you bow

Automatic rakat counting through the watch's barometric altimeter — no taps required during salah. A guided calibration captures your standing-to-sujood drop so detection fits the way you pray. You may choose haptic-only or haptic-with-sound for the final-rakat cue.

It knows the time, and the direction

Twelve calculation methods are supported — Muslim World League, ISNA, Egyptian, Karachi, Umm al-Qura, Dubai, Moonsighting Committee, Kuwait, Qatar, Singapore, Tehran, and Diyanet — with both Shafi and Hanafi schools available for Asr. A live Qibla compass shows the bearing toward Mecca, calculated on the device from your location alone.

It remembers, gently

The last 365 days of your prayer sessions are kept on the watch — daily, weekly, and monthly views, plus a chronological list. You may export the entire history as JSON via the system share sheet. Nothing is uploaded anywhere; what you keep, you keep on your wrist.

It speaks every language you might pray in

Available in eighteen languages: English, العربية, বাংলা, 简体中文, 繁體中文, Français, Deutsch, Hausa, Bahasa Indonesia, 日本語, 한국어, Bahasa Melayu, فارسی, Русский, Español, Kiswahili, Türkçe, and اردو. Right-to-left layout for Arabic, Persian, and Urdu.

— II —Quiet Things

A handful of quiet integrations sit beneath the surface, opt-in and reversible:

  • Optional on-watch notifications at each daily prayer time, scheduled locally.
  • Optional Apple Health workout entry — start time, end time, and prayer name only; toggle off any time.
  • A “Start Prayer” Siri shortcut for a one-sentence quick start.
  • Always-On display supported.
  • Full VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, Reduce Motion, and Series 9 Double-Tap support.

✦   ✦   ✦

— III —On Privacy

Your prayer data lives on your watch and only on your watch. Location is rounded to roughly the nearest kilometre before being saved on-device and is never transmitted; it serves only to compute prayer times and the Qibla direction. There are no accounts, no servers, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking of any kind.

The only path by which any device-local data can ever leave your watch is when you yourself tap “Detailed Bug Report” in Tools → Feedback, which opens the system mail composer pre-filled with a log appendix that you review and explicitly send.

The full Privacy Policy spells it out in detail.
